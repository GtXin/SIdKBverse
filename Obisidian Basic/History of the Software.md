# Obsidian

> From molten ideas to crystallized knowledge.

---

# Overview

Obsidian is a local-first knowledge management and note-taking application built on plain Markdown files. Unlike most modern productivity software, users own their data completely because notes are stored locally on their devices rather than inside a proprietary cloud platform.  [oai_citation:0‡Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/about?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

The software is developed by Dynalist Inc. and has become one of the most influential tools in the Personal Knowledge Management (PKM) movement.  [oai_citation:1‡Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_%28software%29?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

# Name Origin

The name **Obsidian** comes from the volcanic glass known as **black obsidian (黑曜石)**.

The founders use a metaphor:

```text
Ideas
Thoughts
Notes
Questions
    ↓
Connections
Organization
Reflection
    ↓
Knowledge
```

Just as lava cools and solidifies into obsidian, scattered thoughts can solidify into durable knowledge.

In Chinese:

> 黑曜石（Obsidian） = 凝固后的思想

# Before Obsidian: Dynalist

Before creating Obsidian, Shida Li and Erica Xu built **Dynalist**, a web-based outlining application. While working on Dynalist they discovered a key limitation:

> Knowledge is not naturally hierarchical.

Folders and outlines are useful, but ideas behave more like networks than trees.

This realization became the philosophical foundation of Obsidian.  [oai_citation:4‡Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_%28software%29?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

# Birth of Obsidian

## 2020: COVID Lockdown

During the COVID-19 quarantine period, the founders started building Obsidian.

They were dissatisfied with existing solutions such as:

- MediaWiki
- TiddlyWiki
- Traditional note-taking apps

Their goal was to build a tool that felt more like an IDE (Integrated Development Environment) than a note-taking application.  [oai_citation:5‡Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_%28software%29?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Key goals:

- Local-first
- Markdown-based
- Extensible
- User-owned
- Fast

# Core Philosophy

## 1. Local First

Your notes belong to you.

Notes are stored as Markdown files on your device.

Even if Obsidian disappeared tomorrow, the files would remain accessible through any text editor.  [oai_citation:7‡Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/about?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

## 2. Markdown First

Every note is a plain text file.

Benefits:

- Human-readable
- Portable
- Future-proof
- Git-compatible

No proprietary lock-in.  [oai_citation:8‡Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_%28software%29?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

## 3. Linked Thinking

Traditional software organizes information with folders.

Obsidian organizes information through links.

Example:

```markdown
[[Accommodation]]
[[Wavefront]]
[[Myopia]]
```

The result is a knowledge network rather than a filing cabinet.  [oai_citation:9‡Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_%28software%29?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

## 4. Extensibility

The founders intentionally designed Obsidian like an IDE.

Thousands of community plugins extend functionality:

- Databases
- Kanban boards
- Task managers
- Academic workflows
- AI integrations
- Publishing systems

The community ecosystem became one of Obsidian's strongest competitive advantages.  [oai_citation:10‡Ry Walker](https://rywalker.com/research/obsidian?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

# Why Obsidian Became Successful

Most note-taking applications help users store information.

Obsidian helps users develop ideas.

Its core innovation is not Markdown, graphs, or plugins individually.

Its innovation is treating knowledge as a network.

```text
Information
    ↓
Connections
    ↓
Understanding
    ↓
Knowledge
```

This philosophy resonates particularly strongly with:

- Researchers
- Engineers
- Writers
- Entrepreneurs
- Lifelong learners

# Personal Takeaway

Obsidian represents an alternative model of software entrepreneurship:

- Small team
- Strong principles
- User-funded
- Long-term thinking
- No pressure from investors

Instead of optimizing for growth at all costs, the company optimized for durability, ownership, and trust.

The result is one of the most successful independent software companies of the modern productivity era.
